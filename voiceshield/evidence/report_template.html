07 Limitations & scope

  • Advisory, not conclusive. At low fraud base rates a single confident chunk has limited precision; the determination relies on sustained, multi-chunk evidence and should be combined with other controls.
  • Replay detection is wideband only. The replay detector covers consumer-device loudspeaker replay and is not validated for narrowband telephony (replay cues sit above the phone passband).
  • Audio quality bounds confidence. Windows below 12 dB SNR are marked REDUCED and below 8 dB read GREY (insufficient to judge); heavy noise lowers detector confidence.
  • Speaker-consistency is advisory. Any speaker-change flag is reported separately and never folded into the synthetic-speech score.
  • Model scope. Detectors are strongest on the attack families in their training/benchmark set; novel synthesis methods may be under-scored.

A Appendix — glossary

Determination (Green / Amber / Red / Grey)
Green: no synthetic/replay signature. Amber: possible signature — verify. Red: strong signature — verify harder. Grey: audio insufficient to judge (silence / low SNR).
Fused score
Weighted mean of detector scores in [0, 1], floored by the peak-evidence rule.
Mechanism (Consensus / Peak)
Consensus: multiple models agree. Peak: one high-confidence validated detector floors the score so it is not averaged away.
Peak floor
The minimum fused value a detector alone would impose, per its calibrated confidence factor.
SNR gate
Signal-to-noise quality gate: Normal ≥ 12 dB, Reduced 8–12 dB, Grey < 8 dB.
Speaker drift
Cosine distance of the current voice from the call's reference voice; a sustained jump flags a possible speaker change (advisory).
